ARTCILE ON MALARIA

What is malaria
Malaria is a disease caused by parasites that are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected mosquitoes.
Common signs include fever, chills, headache, and flu-like symptoms.

Mosquitos

Mosquitoes are the primary carriers of the malaria parasites.
They bite and can spread the disease to people, especially in areas where malaria is common.

Lacks Of Nets

When people do not use insecticide-treated mosquito nets, they are at a higher risk of malaria.
Mosquitoes can carry the parasites that
cause the disease, and without protection, individuals are more likely to be bitten while sleeping.

Poor Sanitation

Stagnant water sources can become breeding grounds for mosquitoes.
It is essential to manage water properly to reduce mosquito populations.

Death

Malaria can be deadly, especially for vulnerable groups like young children and pregnant women.
Immediate treatment is vital to save lives.

Disease

Malaria contributes to a significant burden of disease in many regions,
affecting health systems and communities.

Cost

The economic impact of malaria is substantial, including healthcare costs and loss of productivity due to illness.
Preventing malaria can reduce these costs significantly.
